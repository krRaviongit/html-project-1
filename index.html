English physicist and mathematician who was the culminating figure of the Scientific Revolution of the 17th century.

He was born on 4 January 1643 and died on 31 March 1727 (aged 84).

While we know that Isaac Newton discovered Gravity, he also made many other scientific discoveries. Newton is also credited with discovering the three laws of motion. The first law, otherwise known as Inertia, is an item that is still and unmoving until a matter of force is applied to it. A great example of this is a swing, it will hang unmoving until a force is applied to it. The second law is that acceleration happens when force is applied to an object. Taking the example of the swing, if someone is sitting on the swing then it requires a greater force to cause movement. The third law states, ‘for every action there is an equal opposite reaction’. Isaac believed that forces worked in pairs when a force is applied in one direction another comes back in the opposite direction.Newton’s cradle is a great example of the third law in practice. Newton also made the discovery that light is made up of a spectrum of colour. The real question is- what did Isaac Newton not do?
